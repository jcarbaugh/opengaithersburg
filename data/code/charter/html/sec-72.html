Sec. 72 - Right of entry of city employees and agents.

Any employee or agent of the city, while in the necessary pursuit of his official duties, shall have the right of entry at all reasonable hours, and after reasonable advance notice to the owner, tenant, or person in possession, upon any premises and into any building in the city. Any restraint or hindrance offered to such entry by any owner, tenant, or persons in possession, or the agent of any of them may, by ordinance, be made a misdemeanor.

Editor's note: For cases restricting right of entry of city officials, see Camara v. Municipal Court, 387 U.S. 523, 18 L. Ed 2d 930, 87 S. Ct. 1727 (1967) and See v. Seattle, 387 U.S. 541, 18 L. Ed 2d 943, 87 S. Ct. 1737 (1967).
